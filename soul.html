Positive Affirmations

If you’ve never used them before then you might feel a bit silly at first but they’ve always helped me and so I wanted to include them here.

You can create your own, be sure to keep them positive and in the present tense - for example “I am welcoming love into my life".
Say them outloud when you can but in your head is just fine too.
I like to choose one for the day or sometimes the week or month if I’m working on a particular issue.


What exactly are postive affirmations?


Positive affirmations are self-affirming phrases that people repeat out loud. Dr Moses says they’re “designed to promote positive thoughts, feelings and attitudes”. The sentences chosen are specific and individual to each person.


Research by psychologists has shown that positive affirmations can help with the following:


  • Reducing stress
  • Increasing physical activity
  • Promoting healthy eating habits
  • Improving relationships
  • Enhancing academic performance

Here are some positive mental health affirmations to get you started:

  1. I know my worth
  2. I am worthy of receiving good things and of accomplishing my goals in life
  3. I am a strong, capable person
  4. I don’t have to give up my hopes and dreams
  5. Negative thoughts do not serve me anymore
  6. I am proud of myself and will continue to strive to do well
  7. I love my body, my mind, my dreams, and my goals
  8. I forgive myself for mistakes I made and I refuse to hold them against myself
  9. All this hard work I am putting into achieving my goals will pay off
  10. Today I will do my best

Great ways to feed your Soul

  • Forgive somebody

    This is the ultimate burden lifted from your shoulders. Forgiveness will help give you energy, carry less stress and even perform daily task more efficiently.

Play with a pup

Patting a pup or playing with a kitten has been shown to reduce blood pressure and stress hormones. Senior dogs tend to be calm, mellow, sweet and lovable, and they’re always so happy to be with you. This, of course, is good for the soul!


  • Move to music

    Moving to music is a great way to instantly feel happier and to spread that positive energy.

  • Practice gratitude

    Get into a daily habit of jotting down everything you are grateful for.

Spend time with a child

A child's world is new and fresh and full of wonder and excitement!


Smile!

  • Regularly do good deeds

    without expecting anything in return, A study found that empathy towards strangers triggers a release of oxytocin and subsequent generosity.

Feel the spray of a waterfall

The negative air ions boost mood.

  • Gaze at the stars

Throw away your negative thoughts

Take five minutes to jot down everything that is bothering you and then tear the paper up into little pieces and throw it away.


Literally throw away those negative thoughts!


You’d be surprised how good this feels (alternatively, you can burn the pieces in a flameproof dish and return the ashes to the earth symbolising letting go of your troubles.)


In a new study, researchers found that when people wrote down their thoughts on a piece of paper and then threw that piece of paper away, they mentally discarded the thoughts as well.


whereas on the other hand, people were more likely to use their thoughts when making judgments if they wrote them down first on a piece of paper and then tucked the paper into their pocket to protect it.

Just Relax

With today’s modern lifestyle we all know how stressful it can be. Between work, family, and social obligations, it can feel impossible to make time for yourself. But it’s important to find the time.


Relaxing can help keep you healthy, in both your mind and body, helping you recover from the everyday stresses that life throws at you.


There are many benefits to keeping your brain and body relaxed. Relaxation balances out the negative mental and physical effects of stress that we all experience every day.


Stress may be a universal part of everyday life, but that doesn’t mean you should let it get the best of you. Take charge and control your stress by taking a short amount of time everyday and RELAX.
